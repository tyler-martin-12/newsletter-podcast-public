# MIT Technology Review

House style:
- US spelling, formal technology journalism.
- Preserve technical precision and speaker titles.

Proper nouns:
- MIT Technology Review reads as M I T Technology Review.
- OpenAI reads as Open A I.
- Anthropic, Claude, Stanford, Elon Musk, Sam Altman, xAI.
- Tarique Mustafa, GC Cybersecurity, Chorology, NexTier Networks.
- Jakub Pachocki, Eric Schmidt, Andrew Sorota, Josh Hendler.

Domain terms:
- AI reads as A I.
- CEO reads as C E O. CTO reads as C T O.
- USPTO reads as U S Patent and Trademark Office when patents are discussed.
- USC reads as U S C.
- DLP reads as D L P. DSPM reads as D S P M.
- IDS/IPS reads as I D S I P S.
- SSL/IPSec reads as S S L I P Sec.
- 4th and 5th read as fourth and fifth.

Homograph cues:
- Agent and agentic refer to software agents in AI coverage.
- Model usually means machine-learning model, not fashion model.

Boilerplate to strip:
- Deep Dive blocks.
- "Stay connected" sign-up copy.
- Sponsored labels when they stand alone.
- Article recommendation lists after the main story.
